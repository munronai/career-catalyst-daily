# Agent Reviews: Archax "TradFi-to-Crypto" Rosetta Stone

## 1. Executive Review (CEO/CFO Perspective)
**Reviewer:** "The Institutional Visionary"
- **Strategic Alignment:** 10/10. This proposal perfectly aligns with our mission to bridge traditional markets with digital asset efficiency. By speaking the language of fund managers, we are actively removing the single biggest barrier to entry for the next trillion dollars of capital.
- **ROI Potential:** High. Documentation is a low-cost, high-leverage way to reduce the sales cycle and developer onboarding friction.
- **Feedback:** "Ensure that we don't just provide definitions, but also emphasize the cost-saving benefits of moving from T+2 to T+0. This is the ROI that will get the CFOs of our clients excited."

---

## 2. Engineering Review (CTO/Lead Dev Perspective)
**Reviewer:** "The Pragmatic Architect"
- **Feasibility:** 9/10. Using MDX and metadata-driven mappings is a clean, scalable way to maintain the documentation without constant code changes to the dev portal itself.
- **Technical Maintenance:** Moderate. We will need a clear process for keeping the `mappings.json` file in sync with our evolving API and regulatory requirements.
- **Feedback:** "The hover-over components and Algolia synonym mappings are solid. However, we should also ensure that our SDKs (like the .NET one) include these translations as JSDoc or XML comments, so the 'Rosetta Stone' is available right in the IDE."

---

## 3. UX Lead Review (Head of Product Design Perspective)
**Reviewer:** "The User Advocate"
- **User Empathy:** 10/10. This proposal demonstrates a deep understanding of the 'Institutional Developer' persona. They aren't 'crypto-curious'—they are 'efficiency-driven' and 'risk-averse.'
- **Clarity & Friction:** The idea of side-by-side workflow comparisons (T+2 vs Atomic) is brilliant. It uses visual storytelling to explain a complex technical shift.
- **Feedback:** "Make sure the 'TradFi' terms aren't hidden behind too many layers. They should be prominent enough that a non-technical Compliance Officer can navigate the docs without feeling lost in the 0x... hex code."

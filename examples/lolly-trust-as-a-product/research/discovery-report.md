# Research: Trust-as-a-Product (ISO 42001) in Hospitality Tech

## Overview
Lolly's achievement of ISO 42001 (AI Management System) accreditation in early 2026 positions it as a market leader in "Responsible AI" for the hospitality sector. This research focuses on the friction points for enterprise clients (Universities and Stadia) regarding AI adoption and how ISO 42001 serves as a differentiator.

## Enterprise Friction Points (The "Trust Gap")

| Stakeholder | Friction Point | Impact | ISO 42001 Mitigation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **University IT/Compliance** | Student Data Privacy & GDPR | Delays in procurement (6-12 months) | Pre-validated data governance and AI risk management frameworks. |
| **University EDI Boards** | Algorithmic Bias in Facial Verification | Rejection of biometric features due to "ethical concerns." | Documented bias testing and accountability metrics in AI development. |
| **Stadia Operators** | Operational Reliability of AI | Fear of AI "hallucinations" or failure during peak footfall. | Systemic lifecycle management and performance monitoring mandates. |
| **Corporate Legal** | Liability for AI Decisions | Unclear legal standing for automated age verification. | Standardised accountability and transparency in automated decisioning. |

## Competitive Landscape: AI Accreditations

| Competitor Type | Current Status (Estimated) | Trust Differentiator |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Lolly** | **First in Sector (ISO 42001)** | **"Trust-as-a-Product": AI is ethical by design.** |
| Traditional EPoS | ISO 27001 (Security) | Focus on data security, not AI ethics/governance. |
| Tech-Forward Startups | No formal AI accreditation | "Move fast" approach, often lacks robust governance docs. |
| Global Tech Giants | ISO 42001 (Early Adopters) | High trust, but lack hospitality-specific deep-tech. |

## Areas of Concern & Opportunity
1. **The "Black Box" Problem:** Universities are hesitant to adopt AI tray scanning if they cannot explain *how* it works to students.
2. **Biometric Backlash:** Use of facial verification for age-restricted sales requires high "Ethical ROI" to be accepted by student unions.
3. **API Transparency:** Enterprise clients want to integrate Lolly's AI insights into their own data lakes but need "Audit-Ready" API documentation.

## Strategic Assumption
By productising ISO 42001 into a "Trust Tier" or "Certified AI" badge, Lolly can reduce enterprise sales cycles by 30% and win 2x more University tenders where ethical AI is a scoring criterion.

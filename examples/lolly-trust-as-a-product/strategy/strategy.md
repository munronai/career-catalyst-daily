# Strategy: Dominating the "Ethical AI" High Ground in Hospitality

## Vision
To transform Lolly from a "Hardware + Software" company into a "Trust Infrastructure" partner. By 2027, every AI-driven transaction in UK hospitality will be measured against the "Lolly Trust Benchmark" established via our ISO 42001 leadership.

## Assumptions
1. **Regulatory Pressure:** Assuming the UK Government will follow the EU AI Act's lead, making AI impact assessments mandatory for biometric systems (like age verification).
2. **University Sensitivity:** Assuming University EDI (Equality, Diversity, and Inclusion) boards have veto power over technology that uses student biometrics.
3. **Competitive Lag:** Assuming competitors (Lightspeed, Zonal, Oracle) will take 12-18 months to achieve ISO 42001 certification.

## Strategic Pillars

### 1. The "Compliance-as-Code" Moat
We don't just give clients a PDF of our certificate. We embed our governance into our API response objects. 
- **Value:** This makes Lolly "un-swappable." If a client builds their reporting around our `X-Lolly-Governance` headers, switching to a non-certified competitor creates a compliance regression.

### 2. Standard-Setting as Marketing
Leveraging my background in **SWIFT/ICC Working Groups**, Lolly will spearhead a "Hospitality AI Ethics Working Group."
- **Goal:** Define the industry standards for "Fair Tray Scanning" and "Transparent Age Verification."
- **Outcome:** We write the rules of the game that we have already won (via ISO 42001).

### 3. "Privacy by Default" Pricing
We offer the "Trust Portal" as a value-add for Enterprise tiers.
- **Monetization:** The standard EPoS manages transactions; the **Enterprise Trust Tier** manages the *reputational risk* of using AI. This justifies a 15-20% premium on SaaS fees.

# Agent Review: User Research (UX Lead Perspective)

**Reviewer:** User Research AI Agent (Simulated)
**Date:** March 24, 2026

---

## 1. User Empathy: A-
The "Quote Precision AI Auditor" addresses a deep user pain point: the anxiety of a shifting quote. The "Transparency Badges" (e.g., "Standard," "High-Entity Count") are an excellent example of "Explainable AI" that builds trust by showing the *why* behind a higher price.

---

## 2. Key UX Strengths
- **Expectation Management:** Informing users *during* intake that their quote is being verified is better than issuing an instant quote that later changes.
- **Transparency Badges:** Reduces the "black box" feeling of AI-driven legal services.
- **Verified Status:** "This is a Verified Fixed Fee" is a high-trust UI signal.

---

## 3. Critical Concerns & Risks
- **The "High-Variance" Waiting Room:** The UX of the "15-minute verification" needs to be flawless. If a user is left in a "spinning state" without clear next steps, they will churn.
- **Cognitive Load for Lawyers:** The "Solicitor Dashboard" must highlight Lawrence's signals clearly. If a lawyer has to re-read the entire document to "verify," the system has failed. It must be a 60-second "Confirm" experience.

---

## 4. Final Recommendation: PROCEED (WITH UX FOCUS)
Ensure the "Verification in Progress" UI is communicative—perhaps show progress bars or "Lawrence is currently analyzing your boundary photos." Focus on the human-AI handoff to ensure lawyers can verify with minimal effort.

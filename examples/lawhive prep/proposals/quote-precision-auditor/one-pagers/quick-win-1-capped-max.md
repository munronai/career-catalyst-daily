# One-Pager: Quick Win 1 — The "Capped Max" Buffer Strategy

**The Problem:** Negative sentiment is driven by "Price Creep"—where a user pays £400 and is later asked for £200 more. This feels like a "bait and switch" to the consumer, even if legally justified by complexity.

**The Solution:** Instead of quoting the "Optimistic Base" price, we analyze historical variance for high-risk categories (e.g., Conveyancing, Probate) and quote a **"Maximum Capped Fee"** upfront, marked as "Subject to complexity review."

**The "Delight" Mechanism:** If the actual work is less complex, we charge the lower amount and proactively message the user: *"Great news! Your case was more straightforward than anticipated. We’ve saved you £150 (25% vs. your initial quote)."*

**The Strategic Value:**
1. **Psychological Anchoring:** We shift the anchor from the "Lowest" to the "Highest" possible price. Anything below the max is a win for the user.
2. **Competitive Integrity:** We only apply this to categories where the "Max" price still sits within the market's competitive range.
3. **Immediate Implementation:** Requires no AI changes; only a logic update to the pricing table for specific practice areas.

---

## 🚀 Impact (Predicted 3-Month Success)
- **40% Improvement** in "Transparency" sentiment scores.
- **20% Increase** in user referral rate (driven by the "Surplus Value" surprise).
- **Zero** "Bait and Switch" complaints for treated categories.

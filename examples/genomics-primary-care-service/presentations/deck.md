# Presentation: Genomics-as-a-Service (GaaS)
### Mainstreaming Genomics for Neighbourhood Health 2026

---

## Slide 1: The Vision
**Unlocking the "Upstream" Potential of Genomics**
*   **The Goal:** Move genomics from an "elite specialty" to a "background utility" for every GP in the UK.
*   **The Vehicle:** A FHIR-compliant GaaS Gateway that integrates directly with EMIS and SystmOne.
*   **The Outcome:** Safer prescribing, earlier cancer detection, and decentralized clinical excellence.

---

## Slide 2: The Data-Backed Problem
**The Cost of "Downstream" Genomics**
*   **Clinical Safety:** ~£2.2 billion annual cost to the NHS from avoidable adverse drug reactions (ADRs).
*   **Inefficiency:** 5-7 years is the average "Diagnostic Odyssey" for rare disease patients due to primary care invisibility.
*   **Siloed Data:** 5PB of Genomics England data remains inaccessible to the clinicians who see the patients daily (GPs).
*   *Source: NHS 10-Year Health Plan & SW Genomic Medicine Service.*

---

## Slide 3: The Solution & Strategy
**The GaaS Gateway: A Digital Bridge to 2026**
*   **CDS-Hooks Integration:** Real-time pharmacogenomic alerts at the point of prescription.
*   **Digital Test Directory:** One-click genomic ordering integrated into EHR workflows.
*   **Neighbourhood Risk Dashboards:** Helping PCNs identify population-level risks (e.g., FH clusters).
*   **Strategic Alignment:** Directly fulfills the "Neighbourhood Health" 2026 mandate by empowering community teams.

---

## Slide 4: Technical Execution
**Simple, Secure, Scalable**
*   **FHIR Native:** Built on HL7 FHIR R4 to ensure universal compatibility with UK GP systems.
*   **Smart-on-FHIR:** Secure, authenticated access via NHS CIS2.
*   **Low Friction:** Designed as a "headless" service—no new logins, no new dashboards for busy GPs.
*   **Why Me?** Bridging the gap between high-science (GEL) and high-utility (Primary Care) through agile API design.

---

## Slide 5: Impact & Success
**Measuring the 2026 Milestone**
*   **Safety:** 15% reduction in genomic-related prescribing errors within the first 12 months.
*   **Access:** 90% of UK GP practices connected via the GaaS Gateway by 2026.
*   **Diagnostic Velocity:** Shaving 6 months off the rare disease diagnostic path.
*   **Next Step:** Launching the PGx Alpha with 5 Pilot PCNs in Q4 2024.

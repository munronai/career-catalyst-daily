# One-Pager: Genomics-as-a-Service (GaaS) for Primary Care

**The Hook:** Transforming genomics from a hospital-only specialty into a "background service" that keeps neighborhood health teams informed, safe, and proactive.

## 1. The Problem
Genomics today is a "pull" system: specialists must know to look for it. In Primary Care, it is almost invisible. This leads to:
*   **Reactive Medicine:** Patients only get genomic testing *after* a crisis (e.g., an ADR or advanced cancer).
*   **Information Silos:** Genomic data is stuck in GEL/GMS labs; GPs see none of it.
*   **The £2.2bn Gap:** Avoidable adverse drug reactions consume massive NHS resources.

## 2. The Solution: The "Genomics Gateway"
A lightweight, FHIR-compliant API gateway that acts as a bridge between Genomics England's data and Primary Care clinical systems (EMIS/SystmOne).
*   **Push Alerts:** Automated "Genomic Alerts" trigger when a GP prescribes a drug with a genetic contraindication.
*   **Decision Support:** Instant "Next Step" recommendations for high-risk patients (FH, Lynch).
*   **Seamless Ordering:** A digital-first genomic test directory integrated into existing GP workflows.

## 3. The Value (Why Now?)
*   **Alignment with 2026 Goals:** Delivers the "Neighbourhood Health" vision by empowering community teams.
*   **Clinical Safety:** Directly reduces ADRs and prescribing errors.
*   **Patient Empowerment:** Genomic insights delivered via the NHS App, driven by GaaS.

## 4. Success Metrics
*   **90% Integration:** Reach 90% of UK GP practices via FHIR gateway.
*   **ADR Reduction:** 15% reduction in genomic-related prescribing incidents in Year 1.
*   **Diagnostic Velocity:** Reduce time-to-diagnosis for rare conditions by 6 months.
